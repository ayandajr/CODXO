Hipparchus: THE GOD OF TRIGONOMETRY

Timeline of Hipparchus' Contributions to Mathematics

190 BCE - Start of Studies

Hipparchus begins his study of mathematics in his early years.

180 BCE - Spherical Trigonometry

Hipparchus makes significant contributions to spherical trigonometry, which laid the groundwork for modern astronomy.

150 BCE - Creation of the First Trigonometric Table

He creates the first known trigonometric table, an essential tool for solving triangles.

146 BCE - Discovery of Precession

Hipparchus discovers the precession of the equinoxes, a critical concept in astronomy and mathematics.

120 BCE - End of Career

Hipparchus concludes his mathematical career, leaving behind a legacy that influenced many future mathematicians and astronomers.
